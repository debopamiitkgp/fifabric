## Thesis

This deal is a distribution play disguised as a technology acquisition. Google Cloud Platform sits third in market share behind AWS and Azure, and its security offering has been its weakest competitive dimension. Wiz solves both problems simultaneously: it brings 40% of the Fortune 100 as customers and a security platform that works across all three major clouds.

The $32B price tag — 45x trailing ARR — only makes sense through the lens of Google's cloud competitive position, not standalone cybersecurity fundamentals.

## Deal Structure Analysis

The transaction is structured as an all-cash acquisition, notable given Google's typically equity-heavy deal preference. This suggests urgency — Wiz had credible IPO plans that would have taken the asset off the table.

Key structural elements:

1. **Reverse break fee**: $3.2B (10% of deal value), unusually high and reflecting regulatory risk given Google's ongoing antitrust exposure
2. **Retention packages**: 4-year vesting for all engineering staff with acceleration triggers tied to product milestones rather than time
3. **Product independence commitment**: Wiz will operate as a standalone unit within Google Cloud for a minimum of 24 months

## Key Risks

**Antitrust scrutiny**: Google is fighting multiple antitrust actions globally. Adding the dominant cloud security platform creates a bundling argument that regulators will examine closely.

**Multi-cloud credibility**: Wiz's value proposition is cloud-agnostic security. If enterprise customers believe Google will eventually deprecate AWS/Azure support, the customer base erodes faster than the acquisition thesis assumed.

**Talent flight**: Israeli cybersecurity talent is exceptionally mobile. Wiz's founding team has already built and sold companies before — the 4-year vesting cliff may not be sufficient to retain key architects.

## Recommendation

The Retention Half-Life Index for this deal suggests a 30-month effective retention period for Tier 1 engineers — below the 36-month minimum needed for full technology transfer. Google should accelerate the integration timeline for core platform components while maintaining the standalone brand.

For the broader market: this deal reprices every cloud security company. Expect secondary transactions in the $1-5B range as AWS and Azure respond with defensive acquisitions.

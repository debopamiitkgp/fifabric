## I. The Valuation Problem

The brain-computer interface sector entered 2026 carrying approximately $13B in aggregate private enterprise value across fifteen-plus companies, anchored by Neuralink at $9B. This figure embeds three structural valuation errors that any capital allocator must decompose before underwriting a position.

**First, TAM inflation.** Sell-side estimates — notably Morgan Stanley's widely cited $400B long-term TAM — aggregate every conceivable neural interface application into a single addressable market without distinguishing regulatory feasibility, reimbursement probability, or the biophysical constraints on information throughput. A bottoms-up TAM build, constructed by indication, regulatory pathway, and realistic penetration curves, yields a materially smaller figure:

| Scenario | TAM (15-year cumulative) | Key Assumptions |
|---|---|---|
| Conservative | $12-18B | Approved indications only: epilepsy (RNS), movement disorders (DBS), spinal cord injury (SCS). 2-5% surgical candidate penetration. |
| Base | $25-35B | Add near-term pipeline: speech restoration, visual prostheses, closed-loop aDBS. 5-10% penetration on approved; 2-5% on pipeline. |
| Aggressive | $50-80B | Full pipeline plus partial healthy-user option value priced at 10-20% probability weighting. |

The gap between $50-80B and $400B is not conservatism — it is the difference between bottoms-up clinical economics and top-down narrative extrapolation.

**Second, directional conflation.** Output assets (brain-to-machine) and input assets (machine-to-brain) have fundamentally different biophysical constraints, regulatory timelines, reimbursement profiles, and acquirer universes. Valuing them on a single multiple is equivalent to applying a uniform EV/Revenue to both a pre-revenue gene therapy and a scaled medical device franchise.

**Third, missing CapEx for electrode degradation.** BrainGate's 20-year longitudinal data (2006-2026) documents a 7% cumulative decline in electrode recording quality per decade. ICMS safety data — 168 million pulses delivered with zero serious adverse events across a decade — validates chronic stimulation durability, but recording arrays face unavoidable material degradation. Any DCF that models BCI revenue as a perpetuity without an electrode replacement or recalibration CapEx cycle is structurally overvaluing the terminal year.

This memo provides an adapted rNPV framework, segmented by five sub-markets, that addresses all three errors.

## II. The Directional Split: Five Sub-Markets

The binary output/input taxonomy is necessary but insufficient. We restructure around five sub-markets, each with distinct throughput constraints, regulatory pathways, and valuation drivers.

### 1. Restorative Communication (Output — Impaired Users)

Brain-to-text and brain-to-speech for patients with ALS, locked-in syndrome, or severe motor impairment. BrainGate's bimanual typing at 22 words per minute confirms the current state of the art — meaningful for patients who have lost all motor output, but bounded by cortical decoding limits. Neuralink's PRIME trial (N1 implant, 12 confirmed implants, ~50 total enrollment across six trials including the new VOICE speech-decoding trial) and Paradromics (FDA IDE received, first-in-human recording completed, 65,536-contact Connexus array) are the primary assets. **Cognitive-ceiling discount: 0%.** For patients with zero baseline communication, even 10 bits/s is transformative. Reimbursement: humanitarian device exemption pathway viable; commercial payer economics require pivotal efficacy data.

### 2. Sensory Restoration (Input)

Machine-to-brain delivery of visual, auditory, or somatosensory information. Science Corp is the reference asset: $1.5B valuation (Series C, March 2026), CE mark targeted mid-2026, NEJM-published 80% efficacy in 38 patients for its retinal prosthesis. Patterned intracortical microstimulation (ICMS) research now demonstrates perception of edges and motion — a pathway toward higher-fidelity visual input. Cochlear Ltd ($15B market cap, ~$1.8B revenue) provides the definitive comp: sensory restoration at scale, ~8x revenue multiple, decades of reimbursement history. **Cognitive-ceiling discount: 0%.** Afferent bandwidth into cortex is orders of magnitude above the ~10 bits/s efferent conscious bottleneck.

### 3. Neuromodulation Therapy (Input)

DBS, responsive neurostimulation (RNS), and spinal cord stimulation (SCS) for epilepsy, Parkinson's, chronic pain, depression, and blood pressure instability. This is the only sub-market with scaled commercial revenue and established reimbursement:

- **Medtronic DBS/Neuro:** $2.56B quarterly neuromodulation revenue. BrainSense Adaptive DBS approved; 40,000+ Percept RC patients implanted. Strategic partnership with Precision Neuroscience (StealthStation integration, January 2026) signals intent to acquire next-generation electrode technology.
- **NeuroPace:** $440M market cap, $100M FY2025 revenue, 77% gross margin. Intractable generalized epilepsy (IGE) PMA supplement filed, expanding addressable epilepsy population. 47% Medicare reimbursement increase secured. SeizureID AI-assisted diagnostics launching H1 2026. **Commercial reality check:** NeuroPace has achieved only 2-3% penetration of surgical epilepsy candidates after a decade on market; referral funnel conversion from epileptologist identification to implant remains the binding constraint.
- **ONWARD Medical:** $194M market cap (Euronext Amsterdam). Empower BP pivotal trial enrolled first patient (n=60, five US sites) — critically, targeting blood pressure instability in spinal cord injury, not walking restoration. European listing discount of 30-50% versus comparable US-listed neuromod. **Commercial reality check:** 6+ years to projected profitability; SCS incumbent competition (Medtronic, Abbott, Boston Scientific) compresses pricing power. ONWARD must demonstrate differentiated closed-loop outcomes to justify premium.

**Cognitive-ceiling discount: 0%.** Neuromodulation delivers therapeutic electrical patterns, not propositional information, to neural tissue.

### 4. Healthy-User Augmentation (Output — Non-Impaired)

The scenario that drives the largest TAM projections and the most speculative valuations: BCI as a consumer computing interface for healthy individuals. This is where the throughput ceiling binds hardest. Conscious propositional output — the kind required for text entry, cursor control, or command execution — is rate-limited to approximately 10 bits/s by well-documented cortical bottlenecks (Szul et al., 2023; confirmed by BrainGate's 22 WPM ceiling). A smartphone keyboard operates at 40-80 bits/s. No healthy consumer will adopt a surgically implanted device that is 4-8x slower than their thumb.

**Cognitive-ceiling discount schedule:**

| Output Mode | Discount |
|---|---|
| Propositional text/cursor (direct competition with keyboard/touch) | 80-100% |
| AI copilot-augmented BCI (predictive completion, intent amplification) | 30-50% |
| Novel-modality interaction (emotional state, spatial navigation, non-propositional) | 0% (but TAM is speculative and small) |

Neuralink's $9B valuation embeds material healthy-user optionality. Under our framework, that optionality is worth 0-20% of the headline figure for propositional output, with upside only if AI copilot architectures can amplify effective throughput by 5-10x — a technical possibility but not a current demonstration.

### 5. Bidirectional / Closed-Loop Platforms

Simultaneous recording and stimulation with real-time adaptive algorithms. This is the highest-value architecture and the scarcest. CorTec (second human implant completed, bidirectional 32-channel, stroke rehabilitation) and Medtronic BrainSense (adaptive DBS, closed-loop Parkinson's management) are in-human. At-home closed-loop adaptive DBS published in Science Advances (February 2026) validates the out-of-clinic paradigm. **Valuation premium: 1.5-2.5x over unidirectional equivalent** for platform optionality — a bidirectional system can address indications across sub-markets 1-3 simultaneously. Axoft's completed first human trial (Fleuron bio-inspired soft electrode material) represents a materials-science approach to chronic bidirectional implantation.

## III. Probability-Weighted Pipeline Framework

### Adapted rNPV for Medical Devices

We apply risk-adjusted net present value (rNPV) with three modifications for BCI-specific economics:

1. **Cognitive-ceiling discount** applied to output sub-markets per the schedule in Section II.4, compounding with standard stage-gate probability.
2. **Electrode replacement CapEx** modeled as a recurring cost every 7-10 years, calibrated to BrainGate's 7% per-decade degradation rate. This is not a failure mode — it is a maintenance cycle analogous to pacemaker generator replacement, and it should be modeled as such.
3. **Platform optionality layer** added on top of indication-specific rNPV: Platform value = (replacement cost of technology stack + IP portfolio + data asset) x P(at least one indication reaches market). This captures the reality that BCI companies are not single-indication drug programs; a Neuralink or Precision Neuroscience electrode array, once validated in one indication, can be redirected to adjacent sub-markets with incremental regulatory cost.

**Where the pharma analogy breaks:** Unlike a small-molecule pipeline, BCI devices iterate hardware in-field (firmware updates, algorithm retraining), generate proprietary neural data assets that compound in value, and face manufacturing scale-up curves rather than COGS-of-goods-sold compression.

### Stage-Gate Probabilities (BCI-Calibrated)

| Stage | P(success to next stage) | Cumulative P(approval) | Source/Basis |
|---|---|---|---|
| Preclinical to FIH | 35-45% | — | Historical neuromod device attrition |
| FIH to Pivotal | 50-60% | 18-27% | BCI-specific: electrode longevity + safety |
| Pivotal to PMA/De Novo | 65-75% | 12-20% | FDA Breakthrough/TAP pathway adjustment |
| PMA to Commercial Scale | 70-80% | 8-16% | Reimbursement + manufacturing execution |

Applied to the current landscape:

**Neuralink** (Restorative Communication + Healthy-User): $9B EV. PRIME study: 12 implants, safety demonstrated. VOICE speech trial initiated. GB-PRIME expanding to UK. Cumulative P(approval) for restorative communication: ~20%. For healthy-user propositional: ~3% after ceiling discount. Platform optionality (replacement cost of 1,024-electrode array + surgical robot + neural dataset from 50+ implants): $1.5-2.5B. **Risk-adjusted range: $2.5-4.5B** vs. $9B headline.

**Synchron** (Restorative Communication — Endovascular): ~$1B EV. COMMAND trial: 6/6 safety endpoints met. FOCUS-CAN launching in Canada (n=10). Endovascular approach eliminates craniotomy risk but trades off electrode density and signal quality. Cumulative P(approval): ~25% (safety profile advantage). **Risk-adjusted range: $350-600M** vs. ~$1B headline.

**Precision Neuroscience** (Platform): >$500M. Medtronic StealthStation partnership (January 2026) is the strongest strategic signal in the sector — Medtronic does not integrate third-party electrode technology without acquisition intent. Wireless chronic first-in-human targeted 2026. Cumulative P(approval) for first indication: ~15%. Platform value elevated by Medtronic relationship. **Risk-adjusted range: $250-500M.**

**Science Corp** (Sensory Restoration): $1.5B (Series C, March 2026). NEJM-published 80% efficacy in 38 patients. CE mark mid-2026. Pure input — zero cognitive-ceiling exposure. Cumulative P(approval): ~35% (strong efficacy data, clear regulatory path). Cochlear Ltd comp (4.4x EV/Revenue, 16.4x EV/EBITDA at A$2.4B revenue) supports 4-8x revenue at scale. **Risk-adjusted range: $800M-1.5B.** Valuation appears fair at current round.

**Merge Labs** (Platform — Speculative): $850M (seed, January 2026). OpenAI-backed. Molecular BCI plus focused ultrasound — non-invasive or minimally invasive approach. No human data. Cumulative P(approval): ~5%. **Risk-adjusted range: $50-150M** vs. $850M headline. The premium is entirely strategic optionality on the OpenAI relationship and a non-surgical form factor.

### Valuation Gap Analysis

The core dislocation: pre-revenue BCI output companies (Neuralink, Synchron) trade at 2-9x the enterprise value of NeuroPace, which generates $100M in revenue at 77% gross margins with an expanding FDA-approved indication set. This gap is sustained by three narratives: (a) healthy-user TAM assumptions, (b) platform optionality, and (c) Elon Musk / brand premium (Neuralink-specific).

Under our framework, the gap compresses significantly once cognitive-ceiling discounts are applied to healthy-user scenarios. Neuralink's risk-adjusted range of $2.5-4.5B implies 50-72% downside from the $9B headline. Synchron's risk-adjusted range implies 0-65% downside. Science Corp, operating entirely in the input regime, appears approximately fairly valued.

The acquisition-multiple precedent is instructive: Axonics was acquired at ~9x revenue for a differentiated neuromodulation franchise. Blackrock Neurotech was acquired by Tether for $200M — a research-tool valuation, not a therapeutic-platform valuation. **No BCI M&A transactions occurred in 2025-2026 beyond Blackrock/Tether.** No BCI IPO or SPAC has completed. The exit environment remains entirely dependent on strategic acquirers.

## V. Key Risks by Direction

### Output Risks (Sub-Markets 1 and 4)

- **Throughput ceiling.** 22 WPM (BrainGate, 2026) remains 4-8x below smartphone input. AI copilot augmentation is the only identified pathway to closing this gap for healthy users; no peer-reviewed demonstration of copilot-augmented BCI exceeding 50 WPM exists.
- **Electrode degradation.** 7% per-decade recording decline (BrainGate longitudinal data) necessitates either replacement surgery or algorithmic compensation. Neither pathway has been validated at commercial scale.
- **Surgical risk-benefit.** For healthy users, any non-zero surgical complication rate (infection, hemorrhage, device migration) is a binding constraint on adoption. Endovascular (Synchron) and minimally invasive approaches may shift this calculus.
- **Reimbursement vacuum.** No payer pathway exists for healthy-user BCI. Humanitarian Device Exemption caps at 8,000 units/year. Commercial payer engagement for restorative communication requires pivotal efficacy data that does not yet exist at scale.

### Input Risks (Sub-Markets 2 and 3)

- **Penetration ceiling.** NeuroPace's decade of commercial experience demonstrates that even with FDA approval, Medicare reimbursement, and clinical efficacy, surgical BCI penetration of eligible patients remains 2-3%. The referral funnel from neurologist identification to epileptologist evaluation to surgical candidacy to implant is lossy at every stage.
- **Incumbent competition.** ONWARD faces Medtronic, Abbott, and Boston Scientific in spinal cord stimulation — incumbents with established sales forces, reimbursement infrastructure, and surgeon relationships. Differentiation requires closed-loop superiority data.
- **Profitability timeline.** ONWARD projects 6+ years to profitability. European-listed neuromod companies historically trade at 30-50% discounts to US-listed peers, compressing available capital.
- **Reimbursement fragility.** NeuroPace's 47% Medicare boost is a tailwind, but CMS reimbursement is subject to periodic review and potential clawback. SeizureID AI diagnostics (H1 2026) must demonstrate referral conversion improvement to justify the AI investment.

### Platform / Bidirectional Risks (Sub-Market 5)

- **Regulatory novelty.** Bidirectional closed-loop systems lack a clear FDA predicate. Each new indication requires a distinct regulatory submission.
- **Data liability.** Neural data generated by bidirectional platforms raises novel privacy, ownership, and CFIUS concerns (see Section VI).

## VI. Geopolitical and Regulatory Considerations

### Regulatory Divergence

| Jurisdiction | Pathway | Timeline Advantage | Key Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| **United States (FDA)** | Breakthrough Device + TAP (Total Product Lifecycle) | Fastest for novel BCI; Neuralink, Paradromics, NeuroPace all have Breakthrough designation | Post-market surveillance burden; 510(k) predicate gap for bidirectional devices |
| **European Union (MDR)** | CE marking under MDR 2017/745 | Science Corp targeting CE mark mid-2026; ONWARD already CE-marked | MDR bottleneck: notified body capacity constraints add 12-18 months vs. pre-MDR timelines; clinical evidence requirements tightened |
| **China (NMPA)** | Fast-track for strategic technologies | Neuracle: first commercial BCI approval (NMPA), IPO-bound. 8-electrode epidural, approved for clinical use. | Data localization requirements; IP enforceability concerns for foreign acquirers |

### China as a Strategic Variable

China designated BCI as a strategic technology in its 14th Five-Year Plan. Three companies warrant monitoring:

- **Neuracle:** First NMPA-approved commercial BCI. IPO-bound. The regulatory precedent — a government approval for a minimally invasive BCI — has no equivalent in the US or EU.
- **StairMed:** RMB 500M (~$70M) Alibaba-led round signals big-tech conviction in Chinese BCI.
- **Gestala:** $21.6M seed for ultrasound BCI — non-invasive, aligned with China's preference for lower-surgical-risk devices.

State-backed funding and faster regulatory approval create a parallel BCI ecosystem. For Western acquirers, the implications are threefold: (a) CFIUS review will apply to any acquisition involving neural data from US subjects, (b) Chinese BCI companies are unlikely acquisition targets for Western strategics but may compress pricing in emerging markets, and (c) dual-listing or cross-border partnership structures (e.g., Medtronic-Precision model) may become the preferred access mechanism.

### European Valuation Arbitrage

ONWARD Medical's Euronext Amsterdam listing trades at a 30-50% discount to comparable US neuromod assets. For a US acquirer, this discount represents a structural arbitrage if Empower BP pivotal data reads out positively — a US-listed or acquired ONWARD would re-rate to US multiples. The $194M market cap against a $25-35B base-case TAM for neuromodulation implies the market is pricing near-zero probability of ONWARD capturing meaningful share.

## VII. Strategic Mandates for Acquirers

### Mandate A: Neuromodulation Incumbent (Medtronic, Abbott, Boston Scientific)

**Objective:** Acquire next-generation electrode and closed-loop algorithm capability to defend $10B+ annual neuromodulation franchises.

**Priority targets:** Precision Neuroscience (Medtronic partnership already signals intent; $529M post-money valuation; wireless chronic FIH 2026 is the catalyst), CorTec (bidirectional 32-channel, stroke rehabilitation, European regulatory pathway), ONWARD Medical ($194M market cap offers asymmetric upside if Empower BP succeeds; European listing discount is a gift to a US acquirer).

**Avoid:** Neuralink at $9B (premium embeds healthy-user optionality that a neuromod acquirer cannot monetize) and Merge Labs at $850M (preclinical, no regulatory pathway visibility).

### Mandate B: Big Tech / AI Platform (Google, Apple, Meta, OpenAI)

**Objective:** Secure neural-data access and input/output interface optionality for future computing platforms.

**Priority targets:** Merge Labs (OpenAI already invested; molecular BCI + ultrasound aligns with non-surgical consumer form factor), Science Corp ($1.5B, but pure input sensory restoration with consumer-adjacent form factor for visual augmentation).

**Strategic logic:** Big tech acquirers can absorb the 80-100% cognitive-ceiling discount on healthy-user output because they possess the AI copilot infrastructure to potentially close the throughput gap. The 30-50% discount for AI-augmented BCI is their entry point, not the 80-100% discount that binds a pure medtech acquirer.

### Mandate C: Sovereign Wealth / Long-Duration Capital

**Objective:** Build portfolio exposure to BCI platform optionality across sub-markets with 10-15 year hold periods.

**Priority approach:** Diversified basket across sub-markets — one output (Synchron or Paradromics at endovascular/high-density recording), one input (Science Corp or NeuroPace for revenue-generating exposure), one bidirectional (CorTec or Precision Neuroscience). Total portfolio cost: $500M-1.5B for meaningful minority positions. Avoid concentration in any single sub-market given the 8-16% cumulative P(approval) at each individual asset level.

### Mandate D: Defense / Government

**Objective:** Secure non-Chinese supply chain for neural interface technology applicable to warfighter augmentation, traumatic brain injury rehabilitation, and intelligence applications.

**Priority targets:** Blackrock Neurotech (now Tether-owned — acquisition of Tether's neurotech subsidiary, or licensing arrangement), Paradromics (65K-contact array, US-based, FDA IDE pathway), Precision Neuroscience (Medtronic relationship provides manufacturing scale credibility).

**Risk:** CFIUS scrutiny will intensify as BCI moves from research to clinical deployment. Any defense-adjacent acquisition of a BCI company with international clinical trial data will require national-security review.
